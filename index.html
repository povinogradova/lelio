Lelio

Six songs. Six poets. One journey through memory, exile, and the refusal of simplification.

The Project

Why this album exists

The album brings together poems by Rose Ausländer, Theodor Kramer, Mimi Grossberg, Paul Elbogen, Jura Soyfer, and Erich Fried — writers whose lives and work were shaped by exile, displacement, and the upheavals of the twentieth century.

What surprised us while working with these texts was how little had aged.

They ask questions that remain painfully familiar:

What does it mean to belong somewhere?

What remains when the ground shifts?

How does one stay internally honest when belonging becomes complicated?

The album presents the poems not as isolated literary works but as parts of a larger inner journey. It does not attempt to explain history or judge nations. Instead, it creates space for reflection — an invitation to sit with complexity rather than resolve it.

The central idea is the refusal of simplification — people reduced to nationality, identity reduced to labels, history reduced to clear narratives.

The Sound

Music from many places

Europe has always been a place of movement — people travelling, settling, carrying their languages and traditions with them, mixing them with what they found. The musicians who made this album are part of that story. Different backgrounds, different routes, different things brought along and left behind. That is where the sound comes from.

The texts shaped the music as much as the musicians did. Each poem suggested something — a tempo, a texture, a feeling that couldn't quite be named but could be played. The musicians heard those suggestions and followed them, finding connections between what the poets had written and what they themselves had always carried. The result is something that belongs to no single tradition, and to all of them at once.

The Voices

Six poets. One century.

Mimi Grossberg
1905 – 1997
Born in Vienna, she emigrated to New York in 1938 after the Anschluss. A poet, prose writer, and tireless cultural organiser of Austrian exile literature in the United States. Her poem Nachtflug captures a moment of overwhelming cosmic awareness during an early transatlantic flight.
Memorial site ↗
Rose Ausländer
1901 – 1988
Born in Czernowitz (now Ukraine), she survived the Nazi occupation hiding in a cellar. She later settled in Düsseldorf, where she spent her final decades writing prolifically. One of the great German-language lyric poets of the twentieth century.
Poems ↗ Biography ↗
Paul Elbogen
1894 – 1987
Born in Vienna, he was a journalist, novelist, and poet who fled to France after 1938 and later emigrated to North America. Der Emigrant, written in exile in France in 1941, blends dark humour with the painful reality of forced displacement.
Austria Forum ↗
Theodor Kramer
1897 – 1958
Born in Niederhollabrunn, Austria, he was a poet of everyday life, working-class experience, and the overlooked. He fled to England in 1939, where he spent the war years in poverty and isolation before returning to Vienna in 1957.
Theodor Kramer Gesellschaft ↗
Yura Soyfer
1912 – 1939
Born in Kharkov, Ukraine, he grew up in Vienna and became one of Austria's most gifted political playwrights and poets. Arrested in 1938, he died in Buchenwald at 26. His work is marked by fierce intelligence, humour, and humane urgency.
Soyfer Gesellschaft ↗ Texts ↗
Erich Fried
1921 – 1988
Born in Vienna, he fled to London in 1938 after his father was killed by the Gestapo. He became one of the most widely read German-language poets of the postwar era — celebrated for love poetry of radical tenderness and sharply political verse.
Erich Fried Gesellschaft ↗

The Musicians

The people behind the music

Polina Vinogradova
Voice
Polina Vinogradova is a UX/UI designer and content strategist with nearly a decade of experience in branding, marketing, user experience, and software interface development. Music has always been part of her life. Together with Simon Reithofer she co-founded the music group Leliafan in 2019, and later Lelio — a new artistic direction rooted in Austrian exile literature.
Instagram ↗
Simon Reithofer
Guitar & Banjo
Simon Reithofer is a musicologist, jazz guitarist, and instrumental pedagogue based in Graz. His musical focus spans jazz of the 1920s–40s and the music of Django Reinhardt, extending into Klezmer, traditional Romani songs, and Arabic music. He has performed across Europe and Central Asia.
Website ↗
Bernd Kohlhofer
Accordion
Bernd Kohlhofer is an Austrian accordionist and pedagogue known for his stylistic range across classical, jazz, and world music. Trained at the Musikschule Gleisdorf and the University of Music and Performing Arts Graz, he performs with ensembles including the Mosaic Trio and the Adana Klezmer Band, combining Klezmer, Tango Nuevo, and Balkan rhythms with modern improvisation.
